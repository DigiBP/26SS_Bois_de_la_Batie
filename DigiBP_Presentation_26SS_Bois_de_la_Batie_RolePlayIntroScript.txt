Here's a tight roleplay script for two people — one plays HR (Anna), one plays the candidate (Luca).

ROLEPLAY SCRIPT — "How it works today"
Duration: ~2 minutes. No props needed, just two people standing.

ANNA: (picks up phone, upbeat) Luca, hi! Great news — we'd like to offer you the apprenticeship. Congratulations!
LUCA: (excited) Oh wow, thank you so much! So what happens next?
ANNA: So I'll send you a form by email. You'll need to print it, fill it in by hand, sign it, scan it, and send it back. Oh — and if you're under 18, your parents need to sign too.
LUCA: (slight pause) I don't have a printer at home...
ANNA: (slightly awkward) You can use one at the library. Just make sure you send it back within the week. (hangs up, turns to desk, muttering) Okay... now I need to find the cantonal VET template... (shuffles through papers or clicks around) Where is that occupation code... (types slowly) Right, commerce apprentice, year two, salary band... let me check the table... okay, CHF 850... now I need to type all of this into the contract. Name, address, start date, duration, working hours, vacation days...
(beat)
LUCA: (walks back over, awkward) Hi Anna, sorry — I sent the form back but I think I filled in the wrong field? My guardian signed in the wrong box.
ANNA: (sighs, forces a smile) No problem, I'll send it again. (turns back, under breath) That's the second time this week...
(beat — both freeze)
PRESENTER (stepping forward, breaking the scene): Anna will spend another three hours finishing this contract, chasing the signature, informing the school, and removing the job posting. Manually. One step at a time. — This is what we fixed.

A few notes for delivery:
Anna should look genuinely busy and slightly overwhelmed — not incompetent, just buried in a broken process. The audience should feel sympathy for HR, not laugh at them. Luca's printer line always gets a knowing reaction. Keep the pace quick — don't let it drag past 90 seconds before the presenter steps in.
